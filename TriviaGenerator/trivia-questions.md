# Trivia Questions

Here is a collection of trivia questions spanning various categories. Each question includes three progressively more specific hints to guide you toward the answer. Try to answer before revealing each hint!

---

## Category: Astronomy

**Question:**
What is the name of the largest known structure in the observable universe, a massive wall of galaxies stretching over 10 billion light-years across?

**Hint 1:** This structure was discovered in 2003 using data from gamma-ray bursts.
**Hint 2:** It is named after a mythological figure associated with great size and strength.
**Hint 3:** The structure shares its name with a character from Greek mythology who was condemned to hold up the sky.

**Answer:** The Hercules-Corona Borealis Great Wall

---

## Category: Music

**Question:**
Which composer wrote a famous piece of music that was first performed in 1913 and caused a near-riot in the audience due to its revolutionary rhythms and dissonance?

**Hint 1:** The composer was born in Russia and later became a naturalized French and American citizen.
**Hint 2:** The piece was written as a ballet score and depicts scenes from pagan rituals.
**Hint 3:** The work is called "The Rite of Spring" and was choreographed by Vaslav Nijinsky for Sergei Diaghilev's Ballets Russes.

**Answer:** Igor Stravinsky

---

## Category: Biology

**Question:**
What is the only organ in the human body that can regenerate itself to its original size even after up to 75% of it has been removed?

**Hint 1:** This organ is the largest internal organ in the human body by weight.
**Hint 2:** It plays a critical role in detoxification, protein synthesis, and the production of bile.
**Hint 3:** It is located in the upper right portion of the abdominal cavity, beneath the diaphragm.

**Answer:** The liver

## Category: World History

**Question:**
Which ancient civilization built the city of Great Zimbabwe, a massive stone complex in southeastern Africa that served as a royal palace and trading hub from the 11th to the 15th century?

**Hint 1:** The civilization thrived during the late Iron Age and controlled trade routes between the interior of Africa and the Swahili coast.
**Hint 2:** The ruins include a large enclosure with walls up to 11 meters high, built without mortar.
**Hint 3:** The modern country in which these ruins are located took its name from this ancient city when it gained independence in 1980.

**Answer:** The Kingdom of Zimbabwe (built by the Shona people)

## Category: Chemistry

**Question:**
What is the only letter of the English alphabet that does not appear in any element's symbol on the periodic table?

**Hint 1:** Every other letter of the alphabet appears at least once as part of an element's one- or two-letter symbol.
**Hint 2:** This letter falls in the second half of the alphabet.
**Hint 3:** It is the letter that comes just before "K" in the English alphabet.

**Answer:** J

## Category: Geography

**Question:**
What is the name of the deepest known cave on Earth, located in the country of Georgia in the western Caucasus mountains?

**Hint 1:** This cave reaches a depth of over 2,200 meters (7,200 feet) below the surface.
**Hint 2:** The cave's name translates roughly to "Crow's Cave" in the local Abkhazian language.
**Hint 3:** It is called Veryovkina Cave by some, but the deepest confirmed cave has a name starting with "K."

**Answer:** Veryovkina Cave (surpassing Krubera Cave, also known as Voronya Cave, in 2018)

## Category: Art

**Question:**
Which Renaissance artist created a famous fresco cycle known as "The School of Athens," depicting the greatest philosophers and scientists of antiquity gathered together?

**Hint 1:** This artist was a contemporary and rival of Michelangelo, who was painting the Sistine Chapel ceiling at the same time.
**Hint 2:** The fresco is located in the Apostolic Palace in Vatican City, in a room known as the Stanza della Segnatura.
**Hint 3:** This artist, born in Urbino in 1483, is often referred to by only his first name and died at the young age of 37.

**Answer:** Raphael (Raffaello Sanzio da Urbino)

---

## Category: Mathematics

**Question:**
What famous unsolved problem in mathematics, first proposed in 1742, states that every even integer greater than 2 can be expressed as the sum of two prime numbers?

**Hint 1:** Despite being verified computationally for numbers up to at least 4 x 10^18, no general proof has ever been found.
**Hint 2:** The problem was originally proposed in a letter to the mathematician Leonhard Euler.
**Hint 3:** It is named after the Prussian mathematician Christian Goldbach.

**Answer:** Goldbach's Conjecture

---

## Category: Technology

**Question:**
What was the name of the first programmable, general-purpose electronic digital computer, completed in 1945 and used to calculate artillery firing tables for the United States Army?

**Hint 1:** The machine weighed about 30 tons and occupied approximately 1,800 square feet of floor space.
**Hint 2:** It used roughly 18,000 vacuum tubes and could perform about 5,000 additions per second.
**Hint 3:** Its name is an acronym that stands for "Electronic Numerical Integrator and Computer."

**Answer:** ENIAC

---

## Category: Mythology

**Question:**
In Norse mythology, what is the name of the enormous tree that connects the nine worlds and serves as the central axis of the cosmos?

**Hint 1:** This tree is described as an immense ash tree that is evergreen and central to Norse cosmology.
**Hint 2:** An eagle sits atop it, a serpent named Nidhogg gnaws at its roots, and a squirrel named Ratatoskr runs between them carrying messages.
**Hint 3:** Its name roughly translates to "Odin's horse," referencing a myth in which Odin hung himself from the tree for nine days to gain the knowledge of the runes.

**Answer:** Yggdrasil
