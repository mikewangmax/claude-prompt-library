# Portmanteaus: "Music" + "Therapy"

Here are some creative portmanteau options that blend **music** and **therapy** to capture the idea of using music to promote healing and well-being:

## Top Picks

1. **Musitherapy** (mew-zih-THAIR-uh-pee)
   *Music + Therapy* — A clean, intuitive blend that immediately communicates the concept. Easy to say, easy to remember, and professional enough for clinical use.

2. **Melodicure** (meh-LOD-ih-kyoor)
   *Melody + Cure* — Suggests that melodies themselves carry curative power. Has a warm, almost poetic ring to it.

3. **Harmonhealing** (har-MON-hee-ling)
   *Harmony + Healing* — Evokes the idea that harmonic balance in music mirrors the restoration of balance in body and mind.

## More Options

4. **Rhythmedy** (RITH-meh-dee)
   *Rhythm + Remedy* — Highlights the therapeutic role of rhythmic patterns — drumming circles, steady beats, and entrainment. Compact and catchy.

5. **Symphonotherapy** (sim-FON-oh-thair-uh-pee)
   *Symphony + Therapy* — Conveys a grander, more immersive approach to music-based healing, as if the full orchestral experience is the treatment itself.

6. **Tunecure** (TOON-kyoor)
   *Tune + Cure* — Short, punchy, and accessible. Works well for consumer-facing brands or wellness apps.

7. **Sonotherapy** (soh-NOH-thair-uh-pee)
   *Sonic/Sound + Therapy* — Leans into the broader concept of sound healing while remaining rooted in musical practice. Has a scientific, clinical feel.

8. **Melodihealth** (meh-LOD-ih-helth)
   *Melody + Health* — Frames music not as a treatment for illness but as a pathway to holistic wellness. Great for preventive and lifestyle contexts.

## Wildcard

9. **Restretto** (reh-STRET-oh)
   *Rest +Retto (Italian musical term)* — A playful, espresso-inspired name suggesting a concentrated dose of musical rest and restoration. Unexpected and memorable.

---

**My recommendation:** **Rhythmedy** strikes the best balance between originality, memorability, and meaning — it's fun to say, instantly suggests both rhythm and remedy, and works across casual and professional contexts. For something more formal or clinical, **Musitherapy** is the safest choice.
